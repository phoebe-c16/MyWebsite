We focus strongly on the plastic pollution and this is because of the sever increase we have seen in its production and it horrific impacts .Mass plastic production first rose its highest between the 1950s and 1970s, where the amount being produced increased twentyfold (Chalmin 2019), followed by multiple increases. Another such increase was from 2011-2016 where plastic production was seen to rise by 20%, creating a 56million tonne growth (Pan et al. 2019) and now around 5.25 trillion plastic particles sail the oceanâ€™s surface (Hohn et al. 2020). Annually the amount of plastic that reaches the ocean is equivalent to a municipal bin lorry dumping its load every minute, this marine plastic debris accumulates in gyres and is found in both the shallowest and deepest parts of the ocean, made possible via systems such as ocean currents and thermohaline circulation. Over the course of transportation, exposure to abiotic factors cause the breakdown of certain plastics into microbeads. These toxic particles of plastic are the primary micro plastics(Pan et al. 2019) and are small enough for marine life to ingest, creating a global pollution issue, which not only effects a great amount of marine life but also people. This is because once ingested by marine life its unable to breakdown and so goes on to pollute the worldwide supply of seafood. It also effects many non-aquatic organisms like marine birds(Donnelly-Greenan et al. 2019). Other primary microplastics come from everyday garments just from being washed, as this causes the release of plastics and goes on into the environment from waste water not being effectively treat(Pan et al. 2019).


Currently only around 5-10% of plastic waste is recovered and recycled, if this does not change in the future the ocean will be 50% plastic and 50% marine life. Plastics are very harmful for marine biota, it was estimated from organisms such as zooplankton to apex predators that over 2,200 separate marine species were found with plastic in their gastrointestinal tract (Hohn et al. 2020), and causes many other problems such as choking, intestinal blockages, starvation, entanglement etc . Plastics also reduce the amount of CO2 absorbed by the ocean from the atmosphere aiding global warming. Plastic is light weight, durable and cost effective which is why it has become very popular over the years. However, for countless reasons it is vital we keep studying plastic and the effects it has, so we can change the way it is used and disposed.
